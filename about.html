FractureAI is an academic research project exploring how deep learning can support fracture detection from X-ray images — making preliminary analysis faster, more accessible, and visually interpretable.

The goal is not to replace radiologists, but to build a tool that can assist in triage, education, and research settings where expert review is unavailable or delayed.

Dataset

FracAtlas Dataset

The model was trained on the FracAtlas dataset, a publicly available collection of annotated X-ray images covering fractures of the upper and lower limbs.

The dataset was split 80/20 into training and test sets, with a fixed raw test set held out for final evaluation. Preprocessing augmentations — gamma correction, image negatives, and CLAHE — were applied to the training images to improve diversity and robustness.

Limitations

Known Limitations

Important constraints to be aware of before using this tool.

Binary Classification Only

The model outputs only Fractured or Non Fractured. It cannot identify fracture type, severity, or precise location beyond what the Grad-CAM heatmap suggests.

Limited Bone Coverage

Optimised for upper and lower limb X-rays. Results on spine, skull, ribcage, or pelvic X-rays may be unreliable and should be treated with caution.

Not Clinically Validated

This tool has not undergone clinical trials or regulatory review. It must not be used for real patient diagnosis or any clinical decision-making.

Image Quality Sensitivity

Performance may degrade on low-resolution, heavily compressed, or non-standard X-ray images that differ significantly from the FracAtlas training distribution.

Medical Disclaimer

FractureAI is developed solely for academic research and educational purposes. It is a university course project and has not been approved, certified, or validated for clinical or diagnostic use by any medical or regulatory authority.

The output of this tool — including classification results, confidence scores, and heatmap visualisations — does not constitute a medical diagnosis and should never be used as the basis for any medical decision, treatment, or patient management.

Always consult a qualified and licensed radiologist, physician, or medical professional for proper diagnosis and interpretation of X-ray imaging.
