How FractureAI Works

A visual walkthrough of how your X-ray goes from upload to a confident fracture classification result.

Pipeline Overview

Six stages from upload to result — completed in under 3 seconds.

1

Upload X-ray Image

Drag and drop or select your X-ray. PNG and JPG formats are supported. Files are processed in memory and never stored permanently on the server.

Multipart / FormData
2

Preprocessing & Normalization

The image is converted to RGB, resized to 224×224 pixels, and cast to float32. Pixel values are kept in the raw 0–255 range to match the training pipeline exactly.

PIL · NumPy · 224×224
3

CNN Feature Extraction

The backbone network scans the image layer by layer, identifying edges, textures, and structural patterns such as cortical breaks and bone irregularities associated with fractures.

ResNet101 · ImageNet weights
4

Classification

The custom head — GlobalAveragePooling → Dense(512) → Dense(256) → Dense(2, softmax) — outputs a probability distribution over the two classes: Fractured and Non Fractured.

Softmax · SparseCategoricalCrossentropy
5

Confidence Score

The predicted class's softmax probability is reported as a percentage confidence score. A score of 90% means the model assigns 90% probability to that class from the final output layer.

argmax · softmax probability
6

Grad-CAM Heatmap

Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping computes gradients of the predicted class score with respect to the last Conv2D layer. The result is overlaid on the X-ray using COLORMAP_JET at alpha=0.3.

Grad-CAM · COLORMAP_JET · α=0.3

Preprocessing Pipeline

Image augmentation and enhancement applied before training.

01

Gamma Correction

Brightens underexposed X-rays using a gamma value of 0.6, enhancing visibility of bone structures in dark regions.

02

Image Negative

Inverts pixel intensities (255 − pixel) to highlight fracture lines that are more visible in the negative representation.

03

CLAHE

Contrast Limited Adaptive Histogram Equalization (clipLimit=3, tileGrid=8×8) improves local contrast across bone regions.

04

Random Two-Transform

Each image randomly receives two of the three augmentations above, increasing dataset diversity and model robustness.
